"What is this?"

This is a website I programmed to disclose the madeleine recipe I was taught by my mentor, or at least the upscaled version of said recipe, not only to highlight my skills as a baker but to show the importance of learning how to program code. My website is a gift to those wanting to make a ton of madeleines and a testimate to the importance programming has in our modern times.

"My Madeleines"

To start it all off; 1. zest 12 freshly washed lemons, keep in mind this is a for a very large recipe. Ensure that you're getting just the skin, stopping around the point where the area you are zesting appears a very pale yellow, nearly white.

We want the zest of the lemons to add the signature flavor to the bake, it would not be 'Classic lemon-scented Madeleines' otherwise.

2. Add 1600 grams of egg after all the lemons are zested and 3. Add 1600 grams of sugar to get this lovely sight:

A sight that could make a vegan weep. The egg provides the base and the sugar is to give our madeleines a sweet taste of course.

4. Mix that all up and afterwards 5. add about 250 grams of pure honey for flavor. A simple bottle of liquid honey from the store will not do well as it will water down the batter, for this it would be best to buy the jars of more compact or 'waxy' honey. 6. Mix this in as well and 7. add 40 grams of baking powder.

The honey is extra sweetener and adds some more moisture to the finished product, and the baking powder helps it our bake rise later to make the madeliene's signature bump.

For this next step you will want to find yourself a microwaveable bowl to put around 8. 1200 grams of butter into and 9. 200 grams of milk and 10. place it into the microwave for a few minutes , just until there are no more chunks of butter floating in the bowl.

We want to get rid of all the chunks to keep the batter consistent, what we don't want is pockets of butter in our finished product.

Next you'll want to 11. combine the two bowls, the one with your batter and the other with the melted butter and mix the two together thoroughly.

You'll need to put some elbow grease into it, especially with a batch this big, but while mixing be careful not to let the buttery mixture spill, it will pool on the top as the egg and sugar mixture is denser.

As the final step for the batter 12. you must chill it down, keeping it in the freezer for at least 24 hours before using it.

Cover the top with plastic wrap and gently pat it down so the plastic is flush with the batter

When you're ready to bake your madeleines place the batter into a plastic bag and cut the corners to squeeze your batter into a special pan with shell-like molds

The pans are essential for the signature shell shape. Luckily they are quite cheap and not too rare. You can easily order one off Amazon.

13. Preheat your oven to 400 degrees and place your madeleine pan on a preheated baking sheet.

14. Bake until golden and big bumps on top spring back when touched, which should be about 11 to 13 minutes.
